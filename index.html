About Me

My Background

Born in Kansas City in 1996, I have lived in Indianapolis since 1998. After graduating from Carmel High School in 2015, I attended the University of Missouri-Columbia (Mizzou) to pursue a degree in Business Administration. I briefly studied in Madrid, Spain during the summer of 2017 and completed my Bachelor's Degree in December of 2018. I moved back to Indy in 2019 and am now engaged to my beautiful fiance, Courtney. We have one dog together, a Beagle mix named Banjo. He's our precious baby!

My Professional Goals

Since my graduation, I have been working for my family business, Insurance Career Training, Inc. We provide educational services (both online and in-person) for individuals who hold insurance licenses: notably insurance agents, financial and retirement planners, funeral home directors, and professionals in related industries. Our e-commerce store is operated through Shopify, and I am seeking experience in web development to increase the user-friendliness and accessibility of our company's online store.

My Hobbies

In my free time away from the computer, I greatly enjoy working with my hands. I own a home and can consistently be found building projects around the house and fixing things up. I greatly enjoy sports, my two favorite teams being the Kansas City Chiefs and the Spanish soccer team Atletico de Madrid. I often travel back to Missouri to visit old friends and visit with Courtney's family in St. Louis. I also play guitar and own a Fender Telecaster - whose colors are the inspiration for the design of this web page!

Examples of My Work

CSS Creature

Using HTML in conjunction with CSS styling, I created a cat-like cartoon. Although not the most aesthetic drawing, this creature utilizes styles such as borders, margins, padding, classes, IDs, z-indexes, and more. The entire code can be viewed using the button below.

Static HTML Layout

A detailed recreation of an actual webpage. This project taught me to better understand the CSS grid system and how classes can help apply consistent formatting across a page. Coding was required for HTML elements such as buttons, headers, and navbars.

Bulbasaur API

My first attempt using Javascript to extract data from an Application Programming Interface (API). This small project displays the weight and height of the Pokemon Bulbasaur as he evolves and grows. This project taught me the value of the console in extracting and displaying JSON data from APIs.

GIF Finder

A much improved attempt at using an API to display content on a dynamic webpage. I further researched DOM manipulation, JSON arrays, and the fetch function to create a search engine using Giphy's API. Using any keyword, a single GIF image and it's source information will display on your screen.
